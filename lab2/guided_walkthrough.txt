Self-check:
The comment says that f modifies ra. Where exactly does f modify ra?
- call g is essentially jal ra, g, the address of the next instruction is stored into ra and the control jumps to g.
- thats how ra is modified without being called explicitly.
- ra is modified, but never saved

Why is the first `mv a0, s0` redundant? Why would it still be beneficial to retain it despite the redundancy?
- because a0 already contains the argument and nothing was modified.
- its still beneficial because it protects against future edits and makes the function structure clearer
